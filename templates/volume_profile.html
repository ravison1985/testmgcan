Volume Profile - User Guide
What is Volume Profile?
Volume Profile: Shows the distribution of trading volume across different price levels over the last 5 trading days. This helps identify where most trading activity occurred and key support/resistance zones.
Horizontal Chart: Each horizontal bar represents the total volume traded at that specific price level across 5 days.
Key Levels Explained
VPOC (Volume Point of Control): The price level with the HIGHEST trading volume in 5 days. This is where traders were most active and often acts as a strong support or resistance level. Shown in red.
VAH (Value Area High): The upper boundary of the 70% value area. This defines the highest price where 70% of the volume was traded. Often acts as resistance. Shown in teal.
VAL (Value Area Low): The lower boundary of the 70% value area. This defines the lowest price where 70% of the volume was traded. Often acts as support. Shown in yellow.
Value Area (70%): The price range between VAH and VAL where 70% of all trading volume occurred. This is the "fair value" zone where most traders agreed on price.
